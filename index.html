Black History Month

Mark Dean

Mark E. Dean is an American inventor and computer engineer. He developed the ISA bus, and he led a design team for making a one-gigahertz computer processor chip. He holds three of nine PC patents for being the co-creator of the IBM personal computer released in 1981

Achievements When
He holds three of nine PC patents for being the co-creator of the IBM personal computer 1981
Dean was named the first ever African-American IBM Fellow. 1996
He and his father once built a tractor from scratch 1968
He was honoured with the Black Engineer of the Year President's Award, and inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame. 1997
Mark Dean graduated from University of Tennessee 1979
He worked in the Computer Science Department at Harvard 1994
